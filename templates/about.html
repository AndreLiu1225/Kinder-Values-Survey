Schwartz's Theory of Basic Values Survey

About this survey

This survey was inspired by the Schwartz's theory of basic values where the 10 basic values are: Self-Direction, Stimulation, Hedonism, Achievement, Power, Security, Conformity, Tradition, Benevolence and Universalism. According to Schwartz, these values are cherished by people of all cultures and can be hekped to explain diversity and conflict in values. After taking this survey, we will be able to measure your scores on these human values and categorize you into 4 dimensions, Openness to Change, Self-Transcendence, Conservation and Self-Enhancement.

More information about the Schwartz's theory of basic values can be found in this link: https://i2s.anu.edu.au/resources/schwartz-theory-basic-values

Our focus

We have created an interactive website to facilitate an exploration into what basic values most Singaporeans hold. That way, we are able to realize the values that shape Singaporean society. On a smaller scale, we would want you to find out and reflect on the values you hold dear, so that you are able to better understand your principles.

Our logo

Purple represents Power(Social status and prestige, control or dominance over people and resources)
Red represents Achievement (Personal success through demonstrating competence according to social standards.)
Yellow represents Conformity (Restraint of actions, inclinations, and impulses likely to upset or harm others and violate social expectations or norms) Orange represents Stimulation (Seeks excitement, novelty and challenge in life.)
Blue represents Security (Prioritising safety, harmony. Pursue stability, whether of society, relationships or oneself.)
Light Purple represents Hedonism (Pleasure or sensuous gratification for oneself.)
Pink represents Benevolence (Preserving and enhancing the welfare of those with whom one is in frequent personal contact (your inner circle))
Green represents Self-Direction (Independent thought and action–choosing, creating, exploring.)
Brown represents Tradition (Respect, commitment, and acceptance of the customs and ideas that one’s culture or religion provides.)
Grey represents Universalism (Seeks to understand, appreciate and tolerate others. Protects welfare for all people and nature.)
